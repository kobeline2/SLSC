This study reexamines the statistical properties of the Standard Least-Squares Criterion (SLSC), which has long been used in Japanese hydrological frequency analysis. Previous studies have already suggested that SLSC depends on sample size and that the conventional fixed threshold is difficult to justify. In this paper, Monte Carlo experiments are extended to six distributions, namely the Gumbel distribution, the generalized extreme value distribution, the log-gamma distribution, the square-root exponential type distribution, the three-parameter lognormal distribution, and the shifted exponential distribution. In addition, two distinct viewpoints in the definition of SLSC are separated: comparison in the original data space and comparison in the standardised plotting-paper space. These are referred to as the X-space and the S-space, respectively. It is shown that SLSC is invariant only under linear transformations, whereas nonlinearly standardised SLSC is no longer equivalent to the original criterion and should be regarded as a different measure. Numerical results indicate that nonlinear standardisation for the three-parameter lognormal model tends to produce systematically smaller SLSC values even when the true distribution is another model. This implies structural unfairness in model comparison. It is therefore concluded that fixed-threshold judgement should be avoided and that, if SLSC is used, model comparison should be carried out consistently in the X-space.
